Ibn Masud

Full Name: ‘Abd Allāh ibn Mas‘ūd; Birthplace: Makkah; Born: 594; Died: 654; Affiliation: Early Muslim; Description: A Companion of Prophet Muhammad and an early scholar regarded as the greatest Qur’an interpreter (Mufassir) of his time. He is known for narrating numerous hadith and for slaying Abu Jahl at Badr. He also fa-mously disagreed with Caliph Uthman over Qur’an standardization; Works: Mushaf Ibn Masud; Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abd_Allah_ibn_Mas%27ud.
